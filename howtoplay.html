Roles:

The Judge Role:

The judge runs the debate and keeps everything fair, focused, and on track. Think of them as the boss of the room.

  • Reviews the case
  • Assigns teams (For/Against)
  • Controls the time
  • Asks tricky questions
  • Rejecting weak arguments
  • Give Penalties
  • Decides the winner

The Players Role:

Players must defend their side using strong arguments, clean speaking, and smart thinking.

  • Two Groups: each group has one girl and one boy.
  • Defender: The person who presents the main argument and defends it with logical details.
  • Attacker: The person who focuses on refuting the opposing team's arguments and raising logical objections.

How to Play?

  • Draw the Case:

    The judge draws a case card and reads it aloud.

  • Assign Positions:

    Judge deciding which team will be For and Against.

  • Draw Action Cards:

    Each team draws only two action cards.

  • Preparation Time:

    Preparation time: 3 minutes per team.
    Teams are allowed to discuss ideas internally without the judge's intervention.

  • Present Arguments:

    "For" Team:

    Clearly presents its main arguments. Push or clarification cards may be used here.


    "Oppose" Team:

    Presents its counterarguments. Push or clarification cards may also be used here.


    Rebuttals:

    Each team responds to the other's arguments.

  • Final Judgment:

    After the discussions have concluded, the judge strikes the gavel and declares the team that convinced him most, or that neither argument is sufficient if he is not convinced by either side.

  • End of Round:

    All unused cards are returned to the pile. The next round begins with a new case and cards.
